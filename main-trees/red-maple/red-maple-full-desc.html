Red Maple (Acer Rubrum)

Basics about Red Maple trees

  • Living:

    The most important aspect about the red maple is its capability to survive in almost any kind of soil type.

  • Height:

    Usually the tree reaches a height of 18-27 meters, and the leaves of a matured tree are 9-11 cm in size. Some species have even shown a growth of up to 35 m.

  • Maturity:

    The tree hits its maturity at the age of 70-80 years. The reason the red maple is able to adapt to a variety of habitats is its nature to suit the soil from a very young age with the help of its roots.

  • Beginner:

    The red maple is among the first trees to bloom in spring.
