Arborvitae (Thuja)

Basics about Arborvitae trees

  • Growing Height:

    Some low-growing shrubs are under 3 feet tall. Large trees can exceed heights of 70 feet and widths of 25 feet.

  • Shapes:

    Mounded, conical, pyramidal, rounded, or pendulous, depending on the cultivar.

  • Foliage:

    Most arborvitaes have flattened, lacy aromatic needles, ranging from emerald green to gold. Clusters of rosebud-shaped cones measuring about Â½ inch long appear in early summer, turning from green to nutmeg-brown.

  • Growth rate:

    Growth rate varies depending on the species and cultivar. Some are very fast growers adding 3 to 4 feet per year, while others, such as dwarfs and miniatures, grow much slower.
